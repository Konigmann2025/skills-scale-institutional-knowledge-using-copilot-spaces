# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

> **Role guidance:** See [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) for who participates and owns retrospective actions. Key roles: **Project Manager** (facilitates), **Engineering Manager** (team health actions), **QA Lead / Quality Engineer** (quality process improvements), **Support / Customer Success** (customer feedback), **Sponsor / Executive Stakeholder** (reviews outcomes at project close).

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload
- Ensure all key roles are represented or have submitted async input (PM, PdM, Tech Lead, Engineering Manager, QA Lead, Design / UX, Support / Customer Success)

## Tracking Improvements
- Add action items to the project backlog or issues with clear owners and timelines
- Review outstanding actions in the weekly PM sync
- Engineering Manager tracks team health action items

## Continuous Improvement Culture
- Measure impact of action items
- Celebrate improvements and make small, iterative changes

## Retrospective Checklist
- [ ] All relevant roles invited or given async input option (PM, PdM, Engineering Manager, Tech Lead, QA Lead, Design / UX, Support / Customer Success)
- [ ] Sponsor / Executive Stakeholder briefed on project closure outcomes
- [ ] Action items assigned with named owners from [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)
- [ ] Previous retrospective action items reviewed for completion
- [ ] Quality process improvements captured and assigned to QA Lead
